Relationship Between pH and the Flow Speed of Water with Aluminum

January 11, 2023

Background

Water is an essential element of life on this planet.

  • composed of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom
  • its special properties help sustain much of the life on this planet
  • pH: the measure of how acidic or basic a substance is, and is based on the concentration of hydrogen ions and hydroxyl ions
  • The scale for pH goes from 0 to 14, with 0 being basic and 14 being acidic (USGS).

The pH of water can affect many of its properties. A study in Beijing showed that the lower the pH in sewer water, the higher the release rate of heavy metals (Li et al., 2013). When the pH is lower, it increases the mobility of the metal and makes H+ occupy more absorption sites, and makes the metals more soluble. Heavy metals also affect the properties of the water they are in.

  • Heavy metals: metallic elements that have a high density
  • Ex: aluminum, lead, and mercury
  • Flow rate: the volume of a liquid (in this case water) per unit of time that passes through a point in an area (Toppr)
  • Most are measured using a flow meter: a machine that usually has some sort of turbine that turns as a liquid passes through it

A study done in the Philippines showed that the highest flow rate was observed during the wet season at low tides, while the lowest flow rate was observed during the dry season at high tides because of the presence of heavy metals (Paronda et al., 2019). I n other words, heavy metals slow down the flow of the water that it is in.

Rationale

A study done in the Georgia Basin showed that the greater the runoff from melting glaciers and snow covers, the more the pH of surface waters will change through natural acidification. One of the big effects of climate change is the melting of glaciers at the poles, and in other areas. Answering the research question in this project is important to show how this change in pH due to the melting can affect the environment because of the possible change in water flow.

Research Practicum

  • Research hypothesis: since heavy metals slow the flow of the water it is in, and the lower pH of liquids dissolves heavy metals, the lower the pH of water with aluminum in it, the higher its flow speed is.
  • Prediction: when the pH of the water with aluminum in it is lowered and put through the flow meter, its flow speed will increase, and vice versa.
  • Independent Variable: the pH of the water
  • Dependent Variable: the flow speed of the water
